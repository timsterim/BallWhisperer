Our Philosophy

Your Sherpa

The ball has lots of stuff going on. It has velocity, spin, trajectory, and a rebound coefficient (how bouncy the ball and paddle are together) that all have a huge effect on the outcome of your ball strike. Navigating all of this to produce good shots is essentially what experience will deliver to all of us. But for those who don't want to wait for that deliverance, getting quality guidance will get you there faster.

A Typical Session

Physics, Kinesiology, Balance and Presence

Sessions start with a gratitude infused evocation. Most instruction focuses on understanding the 'why' behind all aspects of the game which informs our actions and intentions. Concepts like preparation, loading and executing are easier to grasp when you know the why. With the greater understanding, the sessions become live ball drill, often where I am the ball machine putting your ball back in the exact position to apply what we are working on. I put significant effort in modeling the behavior I want my students to exhibit, like split stepping and chasing every ball like your life depends on it.

An Experience vs a lesson

The Neurology of Presence and Gratitude

Eckhart Tolle has suggested that sports deliver a substantial and effective meditative practice. So that autopilot that is executing without you thinking is what presence is all about. You aren't feeling guilty when chasing or hitting a ball nor do you stress about anything other than what is happening at the moment. Some use a lotus position, we chase balls. Mindsets like gratitude enhance brain function. Balance gives the cerebellum extra resources to smack your shot. Balance + Presence = a sweet shot = joy. It's just science!

"Before and after every point or ball in play, be centered, breathe and be grateful that you are playing a game."

Tennis is a game of inches and intentions. These are the core principles we return to again and again — simple ideas that silence the overthinking mind and bring your body back to its natural brilliance.

Tennis has a very combative quality to it. Thus some very deep parts of our brain are triggered which gives us the opportunity to confront and transcend our egos on a point by point basis. We are challenged to be detached from the outcome in favor of the process, yet it is next to impossible to do.

The fitness delivered for those that play tennis regularly comes very easy. When running after a ball with your ego at stake make you forget how gassed and tired you are. Basically free exercise, free endorphins, and it has been reported in some studies that folks who play tennis regularly have a life expectancy 10 years over the average.

The sweetspot in tennis is highly satisfying, thus learning how to get as many sweet hits as possible rewards those with this objective. It's not quite golf in terms of conveyed mechanical advantage, but it is better than most ball hitting sports.

Pickleball rewards patience, soft hands, and smart positioning above all else. These principles will help you slow the game down mentally — so your body can move fast when it needs to.

  • 01
    Get to the Kitchen

    The non-volley zone line is your destination on every rally. The player who controls the kitchen controls the game. Move forward with purpose after every third shot, and you'll win more points than you ever imagined.

  • 02
    The Dink is Your Friend

    Resist the urge to attack every ball. A well-placed dink forces your opponents out of balance. Patience is a weapon — and when you finally do attack, it will mean so much more. Trust the soft game.

  • 03
    Reset Your Paddle Position

    Between shots, return your paddle to center — neutral, relaxed, ready. Tension in the grip kills touch. Loosen your hold, keep the paddle up, and you'll react to anything the other side throws your way.

  • 04
    Play Your Feet First

    Good pickleball is footwork before paddle work. If you're reaching for a ball, your feet have failed you. Practice moving to the ball early, setting your stance, and letting the swing be an afterthought. The ball will thank you.

  • 05
    Silence the Scoreboard

    The score is just information. Every point is its own universe — it doesn't know the last one happened. When you play each rally fresh, free from the weight of the scoreboard, you unlock a performance level that surprises even you.
